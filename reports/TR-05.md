# Technical Report Nº 05 — Acquisition of the Qualitative Database

**Project:** *Forecasting Bitcoin Prices and Analyzing Geopolitical Risks: A Residual Analysis Approach using LSTM* (Geo-BTC).
**Authors:** Erick Cardoso Mendes · Letícia Almeida Aguiar Bezerra · Eduardo Fernandes dos Santos Rocha.
**Phase:** Qualitative research (Phase 2).
**Status:** Closed.
**Companion artefacts:** `data/external/qualitative_analysis.csv`, `data/external/qualitative_analysis.xlsx`.

---

## 1 · Purpose

This report documents the **research process** behind the qualitative
event database used in Phase 2 and presents a **flag-by-flag overview**
of the eight event windows that explain the LSTM residual outliers in
2015. It is the human-readable counterpart of the machine-readable
artefacts under ``data/external/``.

---

## 2 · Research design

### 2.1 · Triggering criterion

The qualitative team was activated after Phase 1 produced
``investigation_periods.csv``. For every outlier detected by the ±2σ
rule, an asymmetric ``[-3, +7]``-day investigation window was opened.
The post-event tail (+7) reflects the empirical lag observed between a
geopolitical event and its market footprint.

### 2.2 · Source corpus

A **convenience-then-replication** sampling procedure was adopted. For
each investigation window the analyst:

1. **Convenience sweep** — looked up the equivalent dates on five
   primary outlets:
   - **CoinDesk Bitcoin Price Index (BPI)** for daily price drivers.
   - **CNN Markets** and **BBC News** for macroeconomic context.
   - **Wall Street Journal** for institutional / regulatory angle.
   - **Bitcoinity.org** for exchange-level volume and liquidity.
2. **Replication** — discarded any candidate event that could not be
   independently corroborated by at least two of the five sources.
3. **Window expansion** — when the literal ``[-3, +7]`` window missed
   a clearly causal event by 2-3 days, the window was relaxed; the
   audit trail is preserved in the *Date* column of
   ``qualitative_analysis.csv``.

### 2.3 · Coding scheme

Each retained event was annotated with five fields:

| Field | Description |
| ----- | ----------- |
| `Date` | Free-form bilingual period string (PT-BR / EN). |
| `Fluctuation` | Direction of the price move during the window — ``Up`` / ``Down``. |
| `Category` | One of eight categories that emerged inductively (see §3). |
| `Event (Macro)` | One- or two-sentence description of the macro narrative. |
| `Breakdown (Micro)` | Detailed breakdown of the proximate market mechanism. |
| `Price Movement` | Open / close prices and percentage during the window. |

Disagreements between analysts were resolved by majority vote among the
three authors; ties (none observed) would have been broken by reverting
to the most conservative source.

## 3 · Inductive category taxonomy

Eight categories emerged from the coding. They are partitioned across
the two behavioural regimes (`Down` and `Up`):

| Regime | Categories |
| ------ | ---------- |
| **Down (negative shock)** | Ecosystem Vulnerability · Global Macroeconomic Shock |
| **Up (positive shock)** | Institutional Interest · Chinese Market Demand Surge · Global Expansion · Technical Consolidation · Speculative Re-entry · Parabolic Move — FOMO |

The down-regime is dominated by **exogenous** shocks (hacks, currency
devaluations) whereas the up-regime is dominated by **endogenous**
market dynamics (volume surges, leverage cascades, FOMO loops). This
asymmetry is itself a finding worth reporting in the paper.

## 5 · Aggregate-flag panorama

The eight windows partition the year into a small number of narratives:

- **Two single-flag down-windows** (March + August) cover the only
  significant negative shocks of 2015 and concentrate ~30% of the
  total absolute residual mass.
- **Six up-windows**, all clustered between July and December, document
  the asymmetric rally that took BTC from ~US$ 210 in August to
  ~US$ 450 in December.
- **Five of the eight windows are China-centred** (yuan devaluation,
  Huobi/OKCoin volume surge, Chinese exchange expansion, FX-protection
  narrative, MMM-Ponzi tail), confirming that 2015 is the year in
  which Chinese exchange dynamics dominated global price discovery.
- The two pure **endogenous** categories — Technical Consolidation and
  Parabolic Move — FOMO — show the highest per-flag MAE values,
  consistent with the hypothesis that LSTM forecasts struggle most in
  regimes driven by reflexivity rather than information shocks.

## 6 · Reproducibility

The full machine-readable database is shipped under
``data/external/qualitative_analysis.csv`` (UTF-8, RFC 4180) and
``data/external/qualitative_analysis.xlsx`` (formatted, with column
widths and frozen header). Both files are version-controlled and
referenced by the merge step in
``src/inference/merge.py`` via the canonical paths declared in
``configs/paths.py`` (``QUALITATIVE_CSV_PATH``,
``QUALITATIVE_XLSX_PATH``).

To reproduce the merge → ER → storyboard pipeline that consumes this
database, run from the repository root:

```bash
make merge   # build inference_table.csv
make er      # compute the Explaining Ratio
make plot    # render the inference storyboard (HTML + PNG)
# or simply
make phase2
```

## 7 · Limitations and next steps

- **Sample size.** Eight windows is small in absolute terms; statistical
  power for category-level conclusions is limited. The Phase-2 design
  is qualitative-explanatory, not inferential.
- **Source bias.** The five-source corpus is heavily Anglo-American;
  CN-language sources (Sina Finance, Caixin) were not systematically
  scanned. A follow-up effort could replicate the procedure on a
  Mandarin corpus.
- **Causal direction.** Containment-based matching does not establish
  causality — only co-occurrence. The paper presents the Explaining
  Ratio as descriptive evidence, not a counterfactual claim.
